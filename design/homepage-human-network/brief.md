## Homepage Job

The homepage must do two things in this order:

1. Establish who Rashed Sabra is: a Head of Product & Technology with an AI engineering background.
2. Give visitors a clear entry into selected thoughts that demonstrate how he approaches trustworthy AI, leadership, and reliable systems.

The homepage is a personal intellectual presence, not a résumé with a blog attached and not an anonymous digital garden.

## Audience

### Primary

LinkedIn readers, professional peers, technology leaders, founders, and collaborators who need to understand Rashed’s identity and point of view quickly.

### Secondary

Readers interested in enterprise AI, automation, product and technology leadership, software quality, and dependable delivery systems.

## Positioning

**Name:** راشد صبرة  
**Role:** `Head of Product & Technology`

**Hero thesis:**

> أقود المنتج والتقنية لبناء أنظمة ذكية تبقى تحت قيادة الإنسان.

**Supporting paragraph:**

> أجمع بين قيادة المنتج والهندسة وخبرة عملية في الذكاء الاصطناعي لأحوّل الأفكار المعقدة إلى أنظمة واضحة، قابلة للفحص، ويمكن للفرق أن تثق بها.

The thesis must communicate leadership, technical depth, and the human-in-command point of view without sounding like a consulting sales page.

## Calls To Action

- Primary: `استكشف أفكاري`
- Destination in phase one: the selected-thoughts section on the homepage
- Secondary: `تعرّف إلى رحلتي`
- Destination: existing `/journey` route
- Contact remains available in navigation or footer but is not the hero’s primary action.

## What I Believe

One authored manifesto follows the hero and explains the worldview behind the writing and professional proof.

**Provisional English copy:**

> I believe technology should expand human judgment, not quietly replace it.
>
> I believe strong product and technology leadership turns complexity into shared clarity: clear decisions, clear ownership, and systems teams can trust.
>
> I believe AI earns its place through evidence. It should be understandable, testable, and accountable to the people affected by it.

The three beliefs carry the existing editorial themes of human-directed AI, product and technology leadership, and dependable systems. They are expressed as a personal point of view rather than category cards or permanent routes.

The section closes with:

> THE BEST WAY TO PREDICT THE FUTURE IS TO CREATE IT.

- Keep the manifesto in English for the current design exploration.
- Treat the closing line as an unattributed adapted statement, not a verified quotation.
- If attribution is introduced later, reverify both wording and source before publication.
- Use one restrained atmospheric space treatment across the section, with an optimized looping video and static poster explored only after wireframe approval.
- The section must feel authored and grounded in Rashed's work, not like generic motivational futurism.

## Visual Thesis

The approved direction is **Human-Directed Knowledge Network**:

- A real portrait is the stable human center.
- A living network represents ideas, systems, and AI.
- Electric teal paths show information and relationships.
- A restrained warm signal marks deliberate decisions and human control.
- The network responds around the portrait; it never replaces, masks, or dominates the person.
- Interaction is concentrated in the hero. The remaining homepage is calm and readable.

## Page Order

1. Navigation
2. Portrait-led hero with identity, thesis, and two CTAs
3. `What I Believe` manifesto with cinematic closing statement
4. Two selected thoughts
5. Compact professional proof
6. Journey invitation
7. Footer/contact

## Success Criteria

- Within ten seconds, a visitor can explain who Rashed is and that the site shares his thinking.
- The portrait and role establish a real person before the AI visual metaphor is decoded.
- `استكشف أفكاري` is the strongest action.
- The belief manifesto sounds specific to Rashed and clarifies the principles behind his work.
- The selected thoughts feel curated rather than generated by a taxonomy.
- The cinematic treatment remains readable and does not compete with the portrait/network identity.
- Professional proof is credible but does not become a homepage CV.
- The result feels personal, modern, technically credible, and memorable.

## Non-Goals

- No redesign of Blog, Blog Post, or Journey in phase one.
- No Build / Think / Learn / Write tracks.
- No knowledge graph, idea map, series system, versions archive, or new public routes.
- No CMS, backend, search, newsletter, comments, or content migration.
- No full-page motion showcase, generic glowing circuit background, Bento-grid identity, or SaaS-dashboard treatment.
- No separate thought-pillar cards and no second standalone motivational quote section.
- No space motif outside the single `What I Believe` section unless a later gate explicitly approves it.
- No copied rocket imagery, signature treatment, wording, or page composition from the Diamandis reference.
- No production implementation before all design gates are approved.
