Every intelligent automation initiative begins with a question more important than the choice of technology: what is actually happening?

A process can look clear on paper while daily practice hides exceptions, approvals, and undocumented routes.

The principle is simple: you cannot automate what you do not understand.

A successful initiative first reveals the real process. It then turns that process into a clear, measurable flow before automation or AI is introduced.

In your experience, what creates the greatest friction: an unclear process, too many exceptions, or resistance to change?
