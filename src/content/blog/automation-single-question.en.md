Once the process is understood, one question remains that technology cannot answer: who owns the outcome?

A process may cross several teams while responsibility remains fragmented.

In that situation, automation accelerates an unresolved decision instead of solving the underlying problem.

Success begins with one owner for the process from end to end, supported by clear criteria for every transition.

That is not bureaucracy. It is a condition for a system that is reliable and accountable.

When a process stalls, what is missing: the owner, the decision standard, or both?
